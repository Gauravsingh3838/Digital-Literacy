
1. Personal Prevention (The Individual Level)The "3-Second Pause": Before clicking any link or downloading an attachment, ask: Did I expect this? If it creates a sense of Urgent Fear (e.g., "Account suspended!") or Urgent Reward (e.g., "Tax refund ready!"), it is likely a scam.Phishing-Resistant MFA: Traditional SMS codes can be intercepted. Switch to Hardware Security Keys (like YubiKeys) or Passkeys. These use cryptographic binding, meaning they physically cannot log you into a fake phishing site.Out-of-Band (OOB) Verification: If you get a suspicious request from a "manager" or "friend," contact them on a different platform. If they emailed you, text them. If they messaged you on WhatsApp, call them.Hover & Verify: On a computer, hover your mouse over a link to see the real destination. In 2026, watch for "Look-alike" domains (e.g., micros0ft.com vs microsoft.com) and QR code phishing (Quishing).2. Professional Prevention (The Organizational Level)AI-Native Email Security: Standard filters are no longer enough. Organizations now use AI detection tools (like Reality Defender or Abnormal Security) that analyze the linguistic sentiment and behavioral patterns of an email to spot AI-generated fakes.Zero-Trust Architecture: Treat every login attempt as a potential threat. Implement Least Privilege Access, ensuring that even if one person is phished, the attacker can't access the entire network.Deepfake Defense Playbooks: Establish a "safe word" or a specific verification protocol for high-value transactions. If an executive calls requesting a wire transfer, the employee must follow a pre-set manual verification step that can't be spoofed by AI voice cloning.DMARC, SPF, and DKIM: These are technical "passports" for emails. Ensure your IT department has these configured correctly so attackers can't easily spoof your company's actual domain.3. What to do if you "Click"Disconnect: Immediately turn off your Wi-Fi or unplug your ethernet cable to stop data exfiltration.Change Passwords: From a different, clean device, change the password of the affected account and any others that share the same password.Scan for Malware: Run a full system scan using reputable antivirus software.Report it: Use your email provider's "Report Phishing" button. This helps the AI learn and protect others from the same campaign.
